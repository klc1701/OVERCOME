HEAR THE UNHEARD

While the nation has come a long way from that days of slavery, African Americans continue to face the discrimination and racism that pervaded the colonies back then, and the fight for equality continues to this day.

ABOLITION OF SLAVERY

Even though Britain had stopped her hand in the slave trade, the practice continued internally in the colonies. By the time the Civil War broke out, anti-slavery sentiment became a pretty popular idea, especially amongst white middle-aged women. A large part in gaining support for abolition was making the horrors of slavery known to the masses to gather sympathy; black and white abolistionists worked together to achieve their goal, as Olaudah Equiano did with the formation of an anti-slavery group, "The Sons of Africa." Freed and enslaved individuals campaigned passionately against slavery and came to fruition with the ratification Thirteenth Amendment.

DESEGREGATION OF THE SOUTH

The prejudice towards black Americans remained for another century, escalating in the creation of the Civil Rights Movement, sparked by the landmark Brown v. Board of Education decision, in which "separate but equal" as unconstituional, overturning the ruling of Plessey v. Ferguson. The decision, though made unanimously, was met with great resistance in the white public and resulted in violent interactions. The desegregation of public schools proved to be a ginat step for the Civil Rights Movement, which only grew from that point. Black students staged sit-ins in diners and boycotted public transportation as a way to force change to come about. Eventually, the goal of the movement expanded and began to call for general equality, particularily politically.

RACIAL DISCRIMINATION BY LAW ENFORCEMENT

Half a centruy after the height of the Civil Rights Movement, tensions have come been called into light by the increased use of social media. The discrimnatory practices of law enforcement when dealing with people of color, especailly black Americans, have recently become exposed. Minorities are more likely to be stopped by police than white people and usually result in altercations. With the emergence of movements like #blacklivesmatter, these incidents have been brought to the public's attention, forcing legitimate investigations when there otherwise would be none.

NINA SIMONE

Nina Simone was a siger songwriter and an active civil rights activist. Her songs often drew upon her ancestry and first directly addresses the inequality African-Americans face in her alblum Nina Simone in Concert, and the civil rights meassage soon became an integral part to her performances.
